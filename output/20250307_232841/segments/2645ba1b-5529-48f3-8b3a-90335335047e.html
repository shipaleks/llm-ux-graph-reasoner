Counsel, Books, and Secrets

Prudence regularly sought counsel in Reverend Edmund Crane, a gentle clergyman quietly wrestling with his fading faith and shadowed past. Edmund's younger sister, Harriet, managed the local bookstore, a warm haven frequented by Eleanor, whose fondness for literature was well-known throughout Glenmoor. Harriet’s shelves prominently displayed a curious and obscure tome titled "The Silent Echo," written by Maxwell Finch—Gregory's estranged cousin—detailing a murder chillingly reminiscent of Eleanor's own mysterious disappearance. Maxwell, reclusive and taciturn, had long insisted the novel was purely fictional, yet whispers persisted among townsfolk who sensed otherwise.
